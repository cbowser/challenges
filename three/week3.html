Helvetica is a sans-serif typeface that was developed in 1957 by Max Miedinger in Switzerland. It was originally called Neue Haas Grotesk, but it's name was changed to Helvetica in 1960, meaning Swiss in Latin. It is known for it's style and readability. It is widely used and can be found on many logos and signs.
